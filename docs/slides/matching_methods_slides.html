Matching and Weighting for Causal Inference

Main Report Narrative for Training Delivery

Author

Luke Heley

Published

March 6, 2026

1 Executive Summary

  • Start with the causal question and estimand, not the method.
  • Judge balance together with overlap, retention, and weight concentration.
  • Exact matching, CEM, and entropy balancing solve different design problems.
Open with the report’s core claim: there is no default winner across methods. The audience should leave with a decision rule, not a favorite algorithm. Emphasize that the comparison is about design tradeoffs, not software output.

2 Why This Comparison Matters

  • These are design-stage tools for making treated and untreated units more comparable before outcome analysis.
  • They improve transparency more reliably than they guarantee validity.
  • A balanced design can still be weak if overlap is poor or key confounders are unobserved.
This slide aligns with the report’s early framing. Stress that matching and weighting are useful because they force explicit decisions about comparability. Also make the limit explicit: none of these methods solves unmeasured confounding.

3 Report Workflow

  1. Define the estimand and focal population.
  2. Choose defensible pre-treatment covariates.
  3. Diagnose raw imbalance and overlap.
  4. Compare methods on the same target.
  5. Estimate effects only after the design is credible.
Walk through this as the report’s organizing logic. If the audience remembers one process point, it should be that outcome modeling comes after design diagnostics, not before them.

4 The Core Tradeoff

You usually cannot maximize all three at once:

  • transparent comparison rules
  • strong observed balance
  • preservation of the original treated population

That tradeoff is the report’s main theme.

This is the design triangle from the report in plain language. Set up the rest of the deck by saying each method sits in a different part of this tradeoff space.

5 Main Worked Example

The main article uses MatchIt::lalonde to estimate an ATT.

  • Focal group: programme participants
  • Pre-treatment covariates include age, education, race, marital status, degree status, and prior earnings
  • Teaching goal: compare balance gains against information loss

Main report

Keep this brief. The purpose is not to reteach the whole dataset, but to remind the audience that the report’s substantive question is the earnings effect for the treated group.

6 What The Main Report Finds

Method Main strength Main cost
Exact matching Most auditable design Residual imbalance on prior earnings
CEM Clearest matched-sample compromise Treated sample shrinks from 185 to 81
Entropy balancing Preserves all treated units Effective control sample falls to about 98.5
Present this as the report’s headline comparison. Use the numbers to make the cost concrete. Do not oversell any one method: exact matching is transparent, CEM is the strongest matched-sample compromise, and entropy balancing is strongest when preserving the treated population is the priority.

7 Main Report Decision Rule

  • Use exact matching when the design is genuinely low-dimensional and auditability matters most.
  • Use CEM when you want an inspectable matched sample across mixed covariate types.
  • Use entropy balancing when retaining the original treated population matters most and the weights stay credible.
This slide translates the report into evaluator guidance. Say explicitly that method choice follows the question and overlap conditions, not analyst preference.

8 Why Add A Second Dataset

The Black Politicians lab tests whether the same logic holds outside the lalonde example.

  • Data: causaldata::black_politicians
  • Training comparison: leg_black
  • Outcome: responded
  • Shared target: ATT

Lab page

Frame this as a stress test of the report’s narrative. The point is not that leg_black is a policy treatment; the point is to teach comparability, overlap, and estimand drift on a second dataset.

9 Black Politicians: Design Problem

  • Raw response rate is 0.393 for Black legislators versus 0.425 for non-Black legislators.
  • Mean district Black share is 0.517 versus 0.063.
  • Democratic share is 0.978 versus 0.501.
  • Raw max absolute SMD across the audited covariates is 2.247.

This is a structural comparability problem, not a small baseline mismatch.

Use this slide to slow down and show why the design burden is real. The audience should see that raw group differences are substantial before any method is applied.

10 Black Politicians: Exact Matching

  • Best used here as a feasibility check, not the preferred final design.
  • It retains all 364 treated units on the chosen discrete covariates.
  • It still leaves open the question of continuous comparability on district composition and resources.
Connect this back to the report’s exact matching discussion. Retention alone is not evidence of a good design. Explain that exact agreement on a few discrete variables can still miss large continuous differences.

11 Black Politicians: CEM

  • CEM keeps the matching logic visible while handling continuous covariates through bins.
  • Max adjusted absolute SMD falls to 0.093.
  • The treated sample falls from 364 to 300.

This is the lab’s clearest matched-sample compromise.

Make the parallel to the main report explicit: CEM again plays the role of the most legible matched-sample design. Note that the cost is visible sample pruning, which shifts the practical target toward treated units with support.

12 Black Politicians: Entropy Balancing

  • Included adjusted mean differences are driven to 0.000.
  • The control ESS falls to about 74.8.
  • The maximum control weight rises to about 209.

The balance table improves, but the weighted comparison becomes fragile.

This is the same caution as in the report, but sharper. Tell the audience that entropy balancing solves the observed mean-balance problem while moving the design cost into a much more concentrated weighted comparison.

13 What Changes Across Designs

  • The simple treat estimate is not stable across raw, exact, CEM, and entropy-balanced designs.
  • That instability is the point of the exercise, not a nuisance.
  • Design diagnostics need to be interpreted before any substantive effect claim.

Evaluated lab output

Use this slide to explain why the report separates design from effect interpretation. If the estimate moves materially when the comparison group changes, the design choice is doing real work and must be defended.

14 Practical Evaluator Takeaway

  • Matching and weighting are useful when rich pre-treatment covariates are available and stronger quasi-experimental designs are not.
  • Better balance is not enough if the design no longer represents the intended ATT.
  • The right question is not “which method wins?” but “which cost is acceptable for this causal question?”
Bring the deck back to the report’s evaluator framing. Mention that matching should sit inside a wider quasi-experimental toolkit rather than act as the default response to observational data.
